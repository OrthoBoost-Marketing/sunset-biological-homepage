Two Paths, One Philosophy

Start With the Bone, Not the Gap

Missing teeth change more than appearance. They change how you chew, how the bone holds its shape, and how the rest of your bite carries the load over the years that follow.

So before anything is placed, we look at what’s actually there. Every case begins with 3D imaging that shows the bone in three dimensions — its height, its width, its quality — because that’s what determines which options are genuinely available to you.

From there the path splits in two: a single tooth, or a full arch. The philosophy behind both is the same.

A Single Tooth

Ceramic Implants, With No Metal at All

When one tooth or a few teeth need replacing, zirconia implants offer a fully metal-free option. They’re biocompatible, they don’t conduct temperature or electricity the way metal can, and the tissue tends to respond well to them.

For patients with metal sensitivities, autoimmune conditions, or simply a preference to keep metal out of the body, this is often the deciding factor. Dr. Mersedeh handles the full process in-house — extraction if needed, bone support, placement, and the final restoration.

Explore ceramic implants
A Full Arch

All-on-X, When Most or All Teeth Are Gone

For patients who have lost most or all of their teeth, All-on-X replaces a complete arch on just a few precisely placed implants — fixed, not removable, and a genuine alternative to dentures that move.

One honest distinction worth making: All-on-X requires titanium implants. Zirconia can’t be used for the implants themselves in a full-arch case. What can be zirconia is the bridge on top — the teeth you actually see — which gives a strong, natural-looking result.

We’d rather tell you that plainly than let the word “metal-free” do work it hasn’t earned.
